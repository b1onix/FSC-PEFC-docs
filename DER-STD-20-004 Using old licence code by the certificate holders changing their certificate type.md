




## DER-STD-50-001_01
## DER-STD-20-011_04
Using old licence code by the certificate holders
changing their certificate type

## Rationale
The  consolidation  and  restructuring  of  businesses     are common
occurrences in  the  corporate  landscape.  Consequently,  numerous
certificate holders are required to transition their operation model from
a  single-site  certificate  to  a  multi-site  certificate,  and  vice  versa.  In
addition,  certain  organizations  may  choose  to  be  part  of  a  group
certification as participating members.
As a result of these changes, organizations are obliged to amend both
their   certificate code and   corresponding   licence   code.   Such
modifications,   however,   may   entail   substantial   costs   for The
Organization, as well as potential environmental impacts arising from
the need to replace printing plates, remove existing product labels, and
undertake related adjustments.
FSC Conclusion 1. FSC grants the right to certificate holders to continue using their
previous  licence  code  for  a  period  of  12  months  following  the
termination of their earlier certification, when the certification type
is changed without  any interruption (i.e., from single to multisite/
group), provided that:
a) The Organization holds a  valid  certification  and a  valid
Licence Agreement for   the   FSC   Certification   Scheme
applicable to the new operation model (single, multi-site or
group); and
b) the previous licence code is used exclusively for on-product
labelling. For any other use, the new licence code shall be
applied.
- The certification body shall verify that, upon expiry of the 12-month
period, the new licence code is applied.
