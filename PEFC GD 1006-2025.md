## Foreword
PEFC, the Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification, is  a worldwide organisation promoting
sustainable forest management through forest certification and labelling of forest and tree-based products.
Products with a PEFC claim and/or label offer assurances that the raw materials that have been used in
their manufacture originate from sustainably managed forests and Trees outside Forests (TOF) areas,
recycled and controlled sources.
The PEFC Council endorses national and regional forest certification systems that comply with PEFC Council
requirements. Systems are subject to regular revisions.
This amended document replaces GD 1006:2012,  PEFC Notification of Certification Bodies operating Chain
of Custody Certification in Countries without Authorised Bodies.
This document is applicable from 1 January 2026. From this date onwards, any PEFC notification fees
applicable for PEFC Chain of Custody certificates issued under the scope of PEFC Council notification shall
be in accordance with Appendix 3 of this document.




## 5
PEFC GD 1006:2025 - Notification of Certification Bodies operating Chain of Custody Certification in Countries without Authorised Bodies
## Introduction
PEFC Chain of Custody certification shall be carried out by third-party certification bodies who are
accredited by accreditation bodies that are signatories of the Multilateral Recognition Arrangement (MLA)
for product certification of International Accreditation Forum (IAF) in order to demonstrate its competence
and independence.
PEFC notification establishes a necessary link between the accredited certification bodies and the scheme
owner (PEFC) and allows certification bodies to issue PEFC recognised certificates.

- Terms and Definitions
3.1 Accredited certificate
A certificate issued by a certification body within the scope of its accreditation which bears the
accreditation body’s symbol.
3.2 PEFC authorised body
An entity authorised by the PEFC Council to perform the administration of the PEFC scheme on behalf of
the PEFC Council.
Note: The authorised body is either the PEFC National Governing Body operating within its country or
another entity which has been authorised by the PEFC Council to administer the PEFC scheme.
3.3 PEFC National Governing Bodies (NGBs)
The PEFC National Governing Bodies (PEFC NGBs) are independent, national organisations established to
develop and implement a PEFC system within their country(ies). A list of the PEFC NGBs and their contact
details can be found on the PEFC website.

- Conditions for PEFC notification
## 4.1 General
The certification body applying for PEFC notification from the PEFC Council shall:
a) Be a legal entity and be able to legally operate in the country/ies where they wish to operate PEFC
certification.
b) Agree to be listed on the publicly available PEFC database including the certification body’s
identification data and/or other data as specified by the PEFC Council.
c) Have valid accreditation, issued by an accreditation body that is signatory of the Multilateral
Recognition Arrangement (MLA) for product certification of the International Accreditation Forum
(IAF) or IAF’s Regional Accreditation Groups:
i. The accreditation shall be issued against ISO/IEC 17065 and the scope of the accreditation shall
explicitly cover PEFC ST 2002, Chain of Custody of Forest and Tree Based Products –
Requirements and PEFC ST 2001, PEFC Trademarks Rules – Requirements in its valid version as
available on the PEFC website www.pefc.org.
ii. The scope of accreditation shall also explicitly state ISO/IEC 17065, PEFC ST 2003 and other
requirements against which the certification body has been assessed.
iii. The accreditation certificate shall be available in English and any other language, as necessary.
iv. If the applicant certification body plans to provide certification against any other PEFC chain of
custody based standard or module standard, the certification body shall also provide evidence
of specific accreditation covering the module, as required by the PEFC Council.
Note: An example of a module standard is PEFC ST 2002-1, PEFC EUDR DDS.
d) Sign a PEFC notification contract with the PEFC Council (Appendix 1).

- PEFC notification issuance procedures
## 5.1 General
5.1.1 To start a PEFC notification process, the PEFC Council shall receive an application according to
Appendix 2 from the applicant certification body by email, including all required documents to
demonstrate compliance with Chapter 4 (Conditions for PEFC notification). The PEFC Council may
replace the form with a digital form.
5.1.2 The PEFC Council secretariat shall carry out a preliminary assessment of the application to ensure
that all the required documents are submitted, and that the information provided is clear.
5.1.3 If any documents are missing or clarifications are needed, the PEFC Council secretariat shall
contact the applicant certification body to request the missing documents or clarification.
5.1.4 The PEFC Council secretariat shall assess the application, and the Secretary General will decide on
the issuance of the PEFC notification of the certification body.
5.1.5 If the PEFC Council secretariat rejects the application, the applicant certification body can appeal
to the PEFC Council Board of Directors to reconsider the decision. The Board of Director’s decision
is final.
5.1.6 If the PEFC Council approves the application, the PEFC Council and the applicant certification body
shall proceed to sign a PEFC notification contract.

- Obligations of the PEFC notified certification body
## 6.1 General
6.1.1 The PEFC notified certification body shall:
a) Have and keep valid accreditation issued in compliance with PEFC ST 2003 and inform the PEFC
Council immediately about any changes in the accreditation. Upon request, it shall provide
updated evidence that it still meets the requirements of valid accreditation.
b) Where the certification body operates through sites or branches, it shall provide PEFC with
information on the different sites or branches, the central office, and evidence that the
geographical scope of the accreditation covers those sites or branches. The certification body
shall also provide information on any changes to the sites of branches, or the central office, and
its accreditation status, immediately and truthfully.
c) Carry out PEFC Chain of Custody certification against PEFC ST 2002 within the scope of the valid
accreditation.
d) Provide the PEFC Council, immediately and truthfully, with a completed reporting form for each
PEFC Chain of Custody certificate and every site covered by a multi-site certificate (including
producer groups), issued within the scope of the PEFC Council notification and on changes
concerning reported certificates,  and summary audit reports.   When a certificate is terminated or
withdrawn, the certification body shall indicate the reason for withdrawal or termination.
e) Provide the PEFC Council, upon request, with a list on its issued PEFC Chain of Custody
certificates, including information on certificate number, name of the certificate holder, country
of the certificate holder, certificate status, certificate holders’ turnover, date of issuance, date of
expiry, date of surveillance audits carried out and products in scope, according to the PEFC
product categories.
f) Pay the PEFC Council the annual PEFC notification fee for every certificate when invoiced by the
PEFC Council. The amount of the fees is specified in Appendix 3, Tariffs of PEFC Notification Fee,
and can be changed by the PEFC Council Board of Directors during the validity of the contract. Any
change to the contract between the PEFC Council and the PEFC notified certification body
resulting from a change in the rates will take effect the year following that in which the PEFC
Council has informed the PEFC notified certification body, in writing, of the change.
g) Agree to be listed on the publicly available PEFC database, including the certification body’s
identification data and/or other data as may be specified by the PEFC Council.
h) Inform PEFC Chain of Custody certificate holders about the range of information it is reporting to
the PEFC Council, and that this information (except the certificate holder’s turnover) will be made
publicly available on the PEFC database.
i) Inform the certificate holder how to obtain a PEFC trademarks usage license.
j) Provide to the PEFC Council any other information related to its PEFC certification activities that
the PEFC Council may request, e.g., audit reports, records of complaints or   internal audit results
limited to the PEFC certification activities.

- Data processing
## 7.1 General
7.1.1 The PEFC Council may collect some personal data on the individuals working for certification
bodies. The personal data collected includes the full name of the contact person, email address
and telephone number. These data are indispensable to ensuring the PEFC certification system
operations, such as to trace the validity of certificates, certification bodies notifications and
accreditation. For the purpose of PEFC certification, email address may be published on the public
search of the PEFC database. If individuals working for certification bodies do not want this
information to be published, we advise to provide generic email addresses.
7.1.2 Personal data on individuals working for certification bodies are kept and stored for a duration of
a minimum of five (5) years, or longer where it is required by the relevant national authority of
the European Member States, and for five (5) years after the end of the PEFC notification contract
validity. Upon request, the PEFC Council may provide data subjects with information about the
personal data it holds. Individuals working for certification bodies have the right to access or
verify their personal data and to have them modified, transferred, corrected, or deleted at any
time. If the individuals working for PEFC notified certification bodies would like to exercise one of
these data protection rights, they can contact the PEFC Council at data@pefc.org.
7.1.3 Further information on PEFC data processing is available from the PEFC Council.
7.1.4 Certification bodies that were already notified by the PEFC Council for Chain of Custody
certification against a previous version of this guidance document shall sign data processing
agreements with PEFC Council.
- Validity of the PEFC notification
## 8.1 General
8.1.1 PEFC notification is valid for the period of the validity of the certification body’s accreditation.
8.1.2 PEFC notification can be terminated or suspended immediately by the PEFC Council if any
provision of the PEFC notification contract is violated.

accreditation.

the validity of the accreditation.
