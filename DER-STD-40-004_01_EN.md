



DER-STD-40-004_01  Economically Integrated Countries treated as a
## Single Entity
## Normative
reference
Clause14.1 b), iv & Clause 16.1 b) of FSC-STD-40-004 V3-1
## Scope
## ☒ Generic
(applicable by all certificate holders)
## ☐ Specific
(applicable only upon individual request and PSU confirmation)
## Approval 09 April 2025
Effective date 11 April 2025
Period of validity Until replacement or withdrawal.
## Rationale
Some   countries   are   economically   integrated   and   share   socio-
economic characteristics, including but not limited to:
- close   diplomatic   relations,   e.g.,   agreements   on   taxation,
customs, shared regulations on timber;
- shared language; and
- use of the same currency.
Certification applicants in countries such as these, e.g., Liechtenstein
and Switzerland, may have sites in both countries, however the current
requirements  in  FSC-STD-40-004  V3-1  provide  that  for  single  CoC
certification, such applicants would not meet the criteria under Clause
14.1 b) iv. Similarly, for group certification, sites across these countries
are unable to be part of a group certification, with reference to Clause
16.1 b), unless these are treated as being in the ‘same country’.
Permitting such countries to be considered as the ‘same country’ would
be  beneficial  for  certification  applicants  with  multiple  sites  across
borders and offer a cost-effective solution.

FSC Conclusion Countries  listed  in  Annex  I  of  this  derogation may  be  regarded  as  a
single  entity and  treated  as  the  ‘same  country’  for  the  purpose  of
obtaining  a  single  CoC  certification  or  for  joining  a  group  CoC
certification.
Annex   I   provides   countries   which are   considered   by   FSC   as
economically integrated and share socioeconomic characteristics.
Any  updates  to Annex  I are  the  prerogative  of  FSC,  dependent  on
receipt  of stakeholder  requests which  include  sufficient  supporting
evidence.
