The <FSC-STD-40-004a FSC Product Classification> provides the product type nomenclatures and codes
that CoC certificate holders shall use to classify their FSC product groups. The document's purpose is to
ensure consistency and accuracy in the identification, control and report of FSC products. This standard
addendum includes  three  sections:  wood  products,  pulp  and  paper  products,  and  non-timber  forest
products  (NTFPs).  Each  section consists  of  a  table  in  which  the  product  group  nomenclatures  are
organized hierarchically (i.e., Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3).
In  the  market,  the Harmonized  System (HS) is  a standardized  numerical method  of  classifying  traded
products.  The  system  is  used in  international trade  and by  customs  authorities to  identify products  and
apply tariffs. An HS Nomenclature (also commonly referred to as HS Codes) has six digits, which are the
same  for  all  countries.  The <HS  Nomenclature  2022  edition> comprises  21  sections,  97  chapters,  and
5612 sub-headings. The wood and articles of wood are classified under section IX, chapter 44. However,
some non-timber products are in different sections or chapters, depending on their nature and composition.
The Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 (EUDR) is the EU deforestation-free regulation that aims to prevent the
import and export of products linked to deforestation and forest degradation. The regulation covers seven
commodities (cocoa, coffee, soy, palm oil, wood, rubber, and cattle) and their derivatives.
The objective  of this document is  to provide additional  guidance to  the organization aiming to align  the
FSC Product Classification with the HS Nomenclature as part of the implementation of <FSC-STD-01-004
V1-0 FSC Regulatory Module>.
NOTE: As the two classification systems have different origins, structures, and purposes, it is often not
possible to make a clear link between them. Therefore, the organization should still periodically consult
the official list of HS Nomenclature (and related support tools) to find the most accurate option. It should
also be noted that an HS code used in the export procedure may be different in the importing country, due
to national specifications.
This document will be regularly updated to ensure continued quality improvement and relevance.
This  is a non-normative document,  and  organizations  are  not  required  to  implement  it  to  demonstrate
conformity to the applicable FSC requirements.
