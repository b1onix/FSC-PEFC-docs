## DER-STD-60-004_01
Exemption from recalculating AAC based on harvestable area and
adjusting management plans accordingly
Normative reference FSC-STD-60-004 V2-0 Criterion 5.2 and related applicable provisions
in Forest Stewardship Standards (FSS)
FSC-PRO-60-004  V1-0  Draft  1-1 Development  of  indicators  for  the
protection of Intact Forest Landscapes considering the landscape level
## FSC-DIR-20-007; ADVICE-20-007-18 V3-0
## INT-DIR-20-007_16
## Scope
 Generic – applicable by:
a) Organizations
i. holding Forest Management certification (FM, FM/CoC, or
CFM) as of the effective date of this derogation; and
ii. having  Intact  Forest  Landscapes (IFL) overlapping  with
their Management Units; and
iii. operating  under  a  license  agreement  by  a  management
plan which: had  been approved before IFL rules became
effective   for   the   Organization by   the   relevant   public
authority – such as state departments or equivalent entities
– and which for that reason cannot be aligned with these
rules within the ongoing harvesting cycle; and
iv. having Management Units located in countries where there
is a registered  pilot for  the development of IFL indicators
based  on  FSC-PRO-60-004 (see Annex  'Countries with
registered Motion-23 Pilots’ below).
b)  Certification bodies of Organizations listed under a).
c)  Assurance Services International (ASI)
## ☐ Specific
(applicable  only  upon  individual  request  and  confirmation by the
FSC Policy & Performance Unit)
## Approval 27 March 2025
Effective date 01 April 2025
Period of validity Until, whatever date is earlier:
a) 31 December 2026, or
b) The  effective  date  of the  applicable Forest  Stewardship
Standard (FSS) resulting from the ongoing Motion 23 pilot.
NOTE: The  FSS  resulting  from  the  ongoing  Motion  23  pilot  test  are
expected to become effective no later than by end of 2026.
Terms & definitions Core area: The portion of each Intact Forest Landscape designated to
contain the most important cultural and ecological values. Core areas
are  managed  to  exclude  industrial  activity (Source: shortened from
## FSC-STD-60-004 V2-1).

Industrial   activity: Industrial   forest   and   resource   management
activities such as road building, mining, dams, urban development and
timber harvesting (Source: FSC-STD-60-004 V2-1).
Rationale The situation of Organizations listed under Scope, point a) is supposed
to be addressed by ongoing pilots under Motion 23/2020 by means of
landscape-level   approaches.   The   enforcement   of   requirements
leading to suspensions or terminations of their certifications would be
against the intent of the Motion.
Nevertheless,   the   relevant   exemption   is   of   limited   duration   in
expectation of relevant results from the pilots being available in 2026.
Derogation 1. The Organization is not required to exclude IFL core areas from the
total area used to calculate the Annual Allowable Cut (AAC) and to
reflect this in its management plan. Actual harvesting rates in the
annual operating area of the Management Unit shall not exceed
levels that can be permanently sustained for that area.
NOTE: This means that harvesting and other industrial activity in
areas  outside  of  the  IFL  core  area  may  continue  as
stipulated by the management plan as long as this does
not lead to harvesting or other industrial activity within IFL
core areas, nor to overharvesting outside IFL core areas.
- The Organization shall obtain independent legal expertise on their
contractual constraints to  adjust  their  management  plans by
excluding IFL core areas from the total area used to calculate the
## AAC.
- The Organization's  certification  body shall  verify  that  the  legal
expertise confirms relevant contractual constraints and has been
provided independently.
- Certification  bodies  and Assurance  Services  International  (ASI)
shall not  issue  non-conformities  related  to  this subject matter
addressed  by INT-DIR-20-007_16,  nor  suspend  or  terminate
certificates based on relevant non-conformities issued before the
derogation’s effective date.
