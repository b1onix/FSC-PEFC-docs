





## ADVICE-20-005-
## 01_V3-0
Reinstating the Annual Administration Fee for all
types of certifications in Ukraine
Normative reference
FSC-POL-20-005 V3-4, Clause 1.4, Section 4, Section 5 and Clause
## 6.4
## Scope
This advice note applies to all certification bodies
Approval date V1-0: 18 March 2022
V2-0: 25 May 2022 (changes applied retroactively)
## V3-0: 05 September 2024
Effective date V1-0/V2-0: 01 April 2022
V3-0: upon approval (valid until revised or withdrawn)
Background FSC had decided to waive the Annual Administration Fee (AAF) for all
types of  certifications  in  Ukraine from Q2/2022  until the  end  of
Q4/2024, regardless of their certification status.
In August 2024, FSC decided to reinstate the AAF for certifications in
Ukraine starting 01 January 2025 in a phased approach.
This Advice Note serves to implement this decision.
ADVICE 1. From  Q2/2022  to  Q4/2024, FSC does not  charge  AAF  from
certification bodies for all types of certifications in Ukraine.
## Phased Approach
- From 01 January 2025, FSC charges the AAF from a certification
body for all types of certifications in Ukraine as follows:
a) within the first year after reinstatement (Q1/2025 –
Q4/2025): 1/3 of the standard calculated AAF;
b) within the second year after reinstatement (Q1/2026 –
Q4/2026): 2/3 of the standard calculated AAF;
c) Third year after reinstatement onward (from Q1/2027):
full standard calculated AAF.
- FSC will continue to waive the AAF for a certification that is fully
suspended in accordance with  <INT-ADV-20-001_02>, due to
being in conflict zones in Ukraine. The AAF for such a
certification will be charged again, in the following quarter when
the suspension is lifted (the determination date marks the
deadline). Depending on the time the suspension has been lifted,
the AAF will be charged according to the phase specified in
Clause 2 a), b), and c), see examples below.
- For a certification that is partially suspended (specific site or
management unit) or has its scope reduced due to being in a
conflict zone, the AAF will only be charged for the sites/MUs

Requirements for certification bodies
- For all valid certifications, the certification body shall update the
Forest Products Turnover (FPT) and/or area in hectare in the
FSC Certification Database (before the determination date of
## Q1/2025 (01 January 2025).
- For a fully suspended certification as per <INT-ADV-20-001_02>
the certification body shall select the following as the reason for
suspension in the FSC Certification Database: “Other - Located
in conflict zone”.
- For a valid certification that is partially suspended or has its
scope reduced as per <INT-ADV-20-001_02> the certification
body shall exclude the FPT/area in hectare from the suspended
sites/management units (MUs).
NOTE: The certification body is responsible for calculating
and entering the adjusted figures (FPT/area in hectare) in
accordance with Clause 2.4 of the AAF Policy.
## Example
Examples for the implementation of phased approach in case of
suspension (see Clause 3 above)
If a certification is suspended due to entering a conflict zone and later the
situation improves, leading to the lifting of suspension, the process for
reinstating the AAF is implemented as follows:
- Suspension is lifted in Q4/2025 before the next determination date:
AAF will be charged from Q1/2026 with 2/3 of the standard calculated
## AAF;
- Suspension is lifted in Q2/ 2026 before the next determination date:
AAF will be charged from Q3/2026 with 2/3 of the standard calculated
## AAF;
- Suspension is lifted in Q4/2026 before the next determination date:
AAF will be charged from Q1/2027 with the full the standard
calculated AAF.
NOTE: This process also applies in the case of repeated suspensions of a
site/MU.
