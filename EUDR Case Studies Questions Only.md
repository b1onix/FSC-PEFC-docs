



PEFC ST 2002-1:2024 Training Materials
## Case Studies

- Input material considerations, processing and output claims

A PEFC certified organisation based in Vietnam, which has achieved a scope
extension for PEFC ST 2002-1:2024, has received the following input materials:

(a) Uncertified material from Vietnamese plantations
(b) FSC Controlled Wood claimed material from Vietnamese plantations

## Questions
- What are the PEFC EUDR Material Categories for these two inputs?
- Which paragraph of section 5 of the Standard describes the information that
shall be obtained for these two inputs?
- For the uncertified material input one of the delivery notes states “mixed
species.” Is this acceptable, and if not, which indicator stipulates the
requirement? What would you do next if you were the certified organisation?
- For the FSC Controlled Wood material, what steps can the organisation take as
part of the EUDR DDS to be sure that the material is no or negligible risk?
- Assume that the organisation successfully processes these materials through
their PEFC EUDR DDS. The materials are then used to make the bases of tables,
which will then be exported for final manufacture and assembly. what will be the
output claim of the table-base products?
- List the information that this organisation will need to pass on to the customer
with this product?

- Exporting into EU. Further processing and sale

The Vietnamese organisation exports the table bases to Portugal.

## Questions
- A Portuguese PEFC certified importer, with PEFC ST 2002-1:2024 in scope
receives the shipment into Lisbon docks. What has the importer done in order to
be able to obtain customs clearance and receive the goods?
- The Portuguese importer successfully receives custom clearance and takes the
shipment to their warehouse and then sells it to a Portuguese furniture
manufacturer. What will the material category be when the table bases arrive at
the furniture manufacturer?
- The Portuguese Furniture Manufacturer is a large company which makes tables
from bases from outside EU, and adds oak and cork table tops sourced from
Portugal. It also has the PEFC ST 2002-1:2024 in scope. Does it need to collect all
the information from the importer regarding the table bases?
- The Furniture Manufacturer is buying oak timber and cork (from oak trees) in
Portugal from a PEFC certified forest. Explain the different input categories that
might apply here?
- Will the oak timber and the cork come with a reference number?
- The manufacturer takes the oak timber and cork through its EUDR DDS. Which
tables does it use and what are the outcomes?
- Assume the manufacturer successfully completes the EUDR DDS and then using
the percentage system creates a table with the Vietnamese bases and
Portuguese oak timber and cork top. What is the output claim?
- Does the manufacturer have to create an EU Due Diligence Statement for this
product?
- Assume that the tabletop comprises 70% of the table by weight/volume. What
label or claim can the manufacturer make?
- The manufacturer now sells a batch of these tables to the Operator at Lisbon
docks who exports them to Sweden, Denmark and Greece, and UK. Sometime
later it comes to light that is a potential serious issue with the non-certified
material in Vietnam that was used to make the table bases. Who needs to inform
who of this situation and what timescales?
- If you were a PEFC COC Portuguese trader who received such a notice what
should you do?
