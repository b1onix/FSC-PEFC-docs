## Rationale
Various  stakeholders  have  reached  out  to  FSC  International  with
concerns that they are unable to meet the requirements in the revised
ADVICE-40-004-23 V2-0 by the end of the given transition date, which
is  applicable  to  all non-FSC-certified contractors  with  outsourcing
agreements in the scope of the chain of custody certification activities.
Additionally,  they  have  noted  their  concern  for  implementing  the
requirement  for  warehouses  that  store  stock  that  is  ‘finished  and
labelled’ but not yet sold, where the property is not owned by or under
the control of the Organization. The reasoning for this difficulty relates
to  the  resource  effort  and  cost  to  implement these  requirements for
potentially hundreds of sites globally, in addition to there being ‘no risk
of mixing’, as the products are ‘finished and labelled’.
In the Chain of Custody Standards Revision,  there is  the suggestion
that  storage  sites  or  logistic  activities  will  be  exempted  from  the
requirements for outsourcing agreements, and therefore any relevant
requirements related to the FSC core labour requirements, provided in
ADVICE-40-004-23   V2-0. Due   to   this potential   change   in   the
requirements forthcoming, which will be initially consulted as part of the
first  draft from  15  November  2025  until 25  January  2026,  FSC
International  is providing this derogation for the provision of storage,
logistics, or forwarding services for ‘finished and labelled’ products, to
provide  time  for  the  analysis  of  the  results  of  the  consultation.
Dependent  on  the  results  of  this  analysis,  the  direction  of future
changes  should  be  clearer by  mid-2026, and FSC  International will
subsequently   provide   further   action,   such   as   extension   of   the
derogation until the implementation of the new standard requirements.
FSC Conclusion FSC  grants  the  right  for a  certified  Organization that  outsources
storage,  logistics,  or  forwarding  services  for  ‘finished  and  labelled’
products to delay meeting the requirements in ADVICE-40-004-23 V2-
0 for any of its non-FSC-certified contractors beyond the transition end
date (31 December 2025),  provided that there  is no risk of mixing of
